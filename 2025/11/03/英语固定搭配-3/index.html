Part 21

take the plunge

  • 💫 释义 : 决定冒险一试 ( 尤指经过深思熟虑后 )
    • 🫧 例句 : After years of hesitation, they finally took the plunge and got married.
    • 🌈 同义 : take a bold step, commit oneself

take by surprise

  • 💫 释义 : 使吃惊, 出乎意料
    • 🫧 例句 : The sudden storm took all the hikers by surprise.
    • 🌈 同义 : astonish, catch off guard, shock

take the floor

  • 💫 释义 : 发言
    • 🫧 例句 : The chairman took the floor to begin the conference.
    • 🌈 同义 : speak, address the audience

take a back seat

  • 💫 释义 : 处于次要地位, 退居二线
    • 🫧 例句 : As he neared retirement, he began to take a back seat in company decisions.
    • 🌈 同义 : assume a less active role, withdraw

take the cake

  • 💫 释义 : 成为最佳/最差者 ( 常含讽刺 )
    • 🫧 例句 : Of all his crazy ideas, this one really takes the cake.
    • 🌈 同义 : be the most extreme, be the limit

take the edge off

  • 💫 释义 : 减弱, 使缓和
    • 🫧 例句 : A cup of tea might take the edge off your headache.
    • 🌈 同义 : reduce, alleviate, soften

take a rain check

  • 💫 释义 : 改期, 下次再说
    • 🫧 例句 : I can’t make it tonight - can I take a rain check ?
    • 🌈 同义 : postpone, reschedule

take the words right out of someone’s mouth

  • 💫 释义 : 说出某人正想说的话
    • 🫧 例句 : You took the words right out of my mouth - I completely agree.
    • 🌈 同义 : express someone’s exact thoughts

take a hike

  • 💫 释义 : 走开, 离开 ( 常作命令式, 不礼貌 )
    • 🫧 例句 : When he refused to leave, she told him to take a hike.
    • 🌈 同义 : go away, get lost, beat it

take a leaf out of someone’s book

  • 💫 释义 : 效仿某人
    • 🫧 例句 : You should take a leaf out of your sister’s book and study harder.
    • 🌈 同义 : imitate, emulate, follow someone’s example

Part 22

take five

  • 💫 释义 : 休息五分钟, 稍作休息
    • 🫧 例句 : We’ve been working hard all morning - let’s take five.
    • 🌈 同义 : take a short break, pause for a moment

take someone to the cleaners

  • 💫 释义 : 骗光某人的钱
    • 🫧 例句 : In the divorce settlement, she really took him to the cleaners.
    • 🌈 同义 : cheat out of money, financially ruin

take the bull by the horns

  • 💫 释义 : 果断处理难题
    • 🫧 例句 : Instead of avoiding the problem, he decided to take the bull by the horns.
    • 🌈 同义 : confront directly, tackle head-on

take with a grain of salt

  • 💫 释义 : 对…半信半疑, 有所保留地听
    • 🫧 例句 : You should take his stories with a grain of salt - he tends to exaggerate.
    • 🌈 同义 : view skeptically, not take literally

take someone under your wing

  • 💫 释义 : 照顾提携某人
    • 🫧 例句 : The experienced lawyer took the young graduate under her wing.
    • 🌈 同义 : mentor, guide, protect

take the wind out of someone’s sails

  • 💫 释义 : 挫败某人的锐气
    • 🫧 例句 : Losing the first round took the wind out of the champion’s sails.
    • 🌈 同义 : deflate, discourage, dishearten

take forty winks

  • 💫 释义 : 小睡, 打盹
    • 🫧 例句 : I’m just going to take forty winks before we go out tonight.
    • 🌈 同义 : nap briefly, have a short sleep

take off

  • 💫 释义 : 起飞; 突然成功; 脱下
    • 🫧 例句 : The plane will take off in ten minutes. / Her career really took off after that movie.
    • 🌈 同义 : become airborne; become successful; remove

take after

  • 💫 释义 : 长得像; 行为像 ( 长辈 )
    • 🫧 例句 : She takes after her mother in both looks and personality.
    • 🌈 同义 : resemble, be similar to

take over

  • 💫 释义 : 接管, 接手
    • 🫧 例句 : The son will take over the family business when his father retires.
    • 🌈 同义 : assume control of, take charge of

Part 23

take up

  • 💫 释义 : 开始从事; 占据 ( 时间/空间 )
    • 🫧 例句 : He has taken up photography as a hobby. / This sofa takes up too much space.
    • 🌈 同义 : begin, start; occupy, fill

take on

  • 💫 释义 : 承担; 呈现; 雇佣
    • 🫧 例句 : She didn’t want to take on more responsibilities at work.
    • 🌈 同义 : undertake, accept; assume; hire

take down

  • 💫 释义 : 记下; 拆除
    • 🫧 例句 : Please take down these important points. / They will take down the old building next week.
    • 🌈 同义 : write down, record; dismantle, demolish

take in

  • 💫 释义 : 理解; 欺骗; 收容
    • 🫧 例句 : It was hard to take in all the information at once. / She was taken in by his smooth talking.
    • 🌈 同义 : comprehend, absorb; deceive; shelter

take back

  • 💫 释义 : 收回 ( 话语 ); 退还 ( 商品 )
    • 🫧 例句 : I take back what I said about him - he’s actually very kind.
    • 🌈 同义 : retract, withdraw; return

take out

  • 💫 释义 : 取出; 带…出去; 消灭
    • 🫧 例句 : He took out his wallet to pay the bill. / I’m taking my parents out for dinner.
    • 🌈 同义 : extract, remove; entertain; eliminate

take away

  • 💫 释义 : 带走; 减去; 外卖
    • 🫧 例句 : The police took him away for questioning. / What’s 15 take away 7?
    • 🌈 同义 : remove, carry off; subtract

take to

  • 💫 释义 : 开始喜欢; 养成…习惯
    • 🫧 例句 : She didn’t take to her new boss immediately. / He’s taken to jogging every morning.
    • 🌈 同义 : develop a liking for; begin a habit

take aback

  • 💫 释义 : 使吃惊, 使困惑
    • 🫧 例句 : I was rather taken aback by his rude response.
    • 🌈 同义 : surprise, astonish, shock

take for

  • 💫 释义 : 误认为是
    • 🫧 例句 : I often take her for her sister - they look so much alike.
    • 🌈 同义 : mistake for, confuse with

Part 24

take against

  • 💫 释义 : 不喜欢, 反对
    • 🫧 例句 : For some reason, the dog took against the postman immediately.
    • 🌈 同义 : dislike, form a dislike for

take apart

  • 💫 释义 : 拆开, 拆卸
    • 🫧 例句 : The mechanic took the engine apart to find the problem.
    • 🌈 同义 : disassemble, dismantle

take along

  • 💫 释义 : 随身携带
    • 🫧 例句 : Don’t forget to take along your passport when you travel abroad.
    • 🌈 同义 : bring with one, carry along

take aside

  • 💫 释义 : 把…叫到一旁说话
    • 🫧 例句 : The teacher took the student aside to discuss his behavior.
    • 🌈 同义 : speak to privately, pull aside

take through

  • 💫 释义 : 引导, 讲解
    • 🫧 例句 : Let me take you through the main points of the proposal.
    • 🌈 同义 : guide through, explain step by step

take upon oneself

  • 💫 释义 : 主动承担 ( 责任 )
    • 🫧 例句 : She took it upon herself to organize the office party.
    • 🌈 同义 : assume responsibility voluntarily

bring about

  • 💫 释义 : 引起, 导致
    • 🫧 例句 : The new policy brought about significant changes in the industry.
    • 🌈 同义 : cause something to happen

bring up

  • 💫 释义 : 提出; 养育
    • 🫧 例句 : She brought up an important point during the meeting.
    • 🌈 同义 : mention a topic; raise a child

bring out

  • 💫 释义 : 使显现; 出版
    • 🫧 例句 : The crisis brought out the best in him.
    • 🌈 同义 : reveal a quality; publish a book

bring forward

  • 💫 释义 : 提出; 提前
    • 🫧 例句 : He brought forward a new proposal.
    • 🌈 同义 : present an idea; move to an earlier time

Part 25

bring down

  • 💫 释义 : 降低; 推翻
    • 🫧 例句 : The scandal brought down the government.
    • 🌈 同义 : reduce something; cause to fall

bring along

  • 💫 释义 : 带来; 促进成长
    • 🫧 例句 : You can bring along a friend to the party.
    • 🌈 同义 : bring someone or something with you; help someone develop

bring off

  • 💫 释义 : 成功完成
    • 🫧 例句 : It was a difficult task, but she brought it off.
    • 🌈 同义 : succeed in doing something difficult

thanks to

  • 💫 释义 : 由于, 多亏
    • 🫧 例句 : Thanks to your help, we finished the project on time.
    • 🌈 同义 : because of, with the help of

no thanks to

  • 💫 释义 : 并非由于 ( 尽管有阻碍 )
    • 🫧 例句 : We managed to arrive on time, no thanks to the terrible traffic.
    • 🌈 同义 : despite, not assisted by

blame for

  • 💫 释义 : 因…责备, 归咎于
    • 🫧 例句 : You can’t blame him for the accident.
    • 🌈 同义 : hold responsible for a fault

be to blame for

  • 💫 释义 : 对…应负责任
    • 🫧 例句 : The faulty wiring is to blame for the fire.
    • 🌈 同义 : be responsible for something bad

in charge of

  • 💫 释义 : 负责, 掌管
    • 🫧 例句 : She is in charge of the marketing department.
    • 🌈 同义 : having responsibility for or control over

free of charge

  • 💫 释义 : 免费
    • 🫧 例句 : The samples were provided free of charge.
    • 🌈 同义 : without any cost

charge with

  • 💫 释义 : 指控, 控告
    • 🫧 例句 : The man was charged with robbery.
    • 🌈 同义 : accuse formally of a crime

Part 26

take charge

  • 💫 释义 : 开始管理, 接管
    • 🫧 例句 : When the manager fell ill, her assistant had to take charge.
    • 🌈 同义 : assume control or command

charge for

  • 💫 释义 : 为…收费
    • 🫧 例句 : How much do you charge for a haircut?
    • 🌈 同义 : ask in payment for

be charged with

  • 💫 释义 : 被指控; 被赋予 ( 责任 )
    • 🫧 例句 : He was charged with murder. / The committee is charged with investigating the incident.
    • 🌈 同义 : be accused of a crime; be given a duty or responsibility

charge at

  • 💫 释义 : 冲向, 向…冲去
    • 🫧 例句 : The bull charged at the matador.
    • 🌈 同义 : rush forward in attack

on a charge of

  • 💫 释义 : 因…罪名而被控
    • 🫧 例句 : He was arrested on a charge of theft.
    • 🌈 同义 : being accused with a specific crime

put in charge of

  • 💫 释义 : 被任命负责
    • 🫧 例句 : She was put in charge of the new project.
    • 🌈 同义 : be given responsibility for

under the charge of

  • 💫 释义 : 在…的掌管之下
    • 🫧 例句 : The department is under the charge of MrSmith.
    • 🌈 同义 : under the management or supervision of

play a role in

  • 💫 释义 : 在…中扮演角色; 在…中起作用
    • 🫧 例句 : Education plays a crucial role in shaping one’s future.
    • 🌈 同义 : to be involved in and have an effect on

have a role in

  • 💫 释义 : 在…中有一份职责; 参与
    • 🫧 例句 : She has a key role in the upcoming project.
    • 🌈 同义 : to have a function or responsibility in

far away from

  • 💫 释义 : 离…很远
    • 🫧 例句 : The village is far away from the noisy city.
    • 🌈 同义 : at a great distance from

Part 27

break away from

  • 💫 释义 : 脱离; 摆脱
    • 🫧 例句 : The province sought to break away from the central government.
    • 🌈 同义 : to escape from or leave someone or something

turn away from

  • 💫 释义 : 对…感到厌恶; 回避
    • 🫧 例句 : He turned away from the violent scene in disgust.
    • 🌈 同义 : to stop supporting or being involved with

get away from

  • 💫 释义 : 逃离; 摆脱
    • 🫧 例句 : She decided to get away from her stressful job for a while.
    • 🌈 同义 : to leave or escape from a person or place

as … put it

  • 💫 释义 : 正如…所说/所言
    • 🫧 例句 : The situation was, as the chairman put it, “a grave threat to national security”.
    • 🌈 同义 : in the words of…, to quote…, as… said, phrasing it in the words of…

news feed

  • 💫 释义 : 信息流; 新闻推送
    • 🫧 例句 : I scroll through my social media news feed every morning to catch up on updates.
    • 🌈 同义 : social media feed, timeline, stream, updates

go through

  • 💫 释义 : 经历, 遭受 ( 困难或不快的事 )
    • 🫧 例句 : She went through a lot of difficulties after losing her job.
    • 🌈 同义 : experience, endure, undergo, suffer
  • 💫 释义 : 仔细检查; 翻阅
    • 🫧 例句 : The lawyer went through all the documents carefully before the trial.
    • 🌈 同义 : examine, check, look through, scrutinize
  • 💫 释义 : 通过, 被批准
    • 🫧 例句 : We’re waiting for our planning permission to go through.
    • 🌈 同义 : be approved, be accepted, be ratified
  • 💫 释义 : 用完, 耗尽
    • 🫧 例句 : We went through a whole jar of coffee in just one week.
    • 🌈 同义 : use up, consume, exhaust, run through

expect sth of sb

  • 💫 释义 : 对某人抱有…的期望
    • 🫧 例句 : The teacher expects hard work of all her students.
    • 🌈 同义 : to require or demand something from someone; to have expectations of someone

account for

  • 💫 释义 : 解释, 说明 ( 原因或理由 )
    • 🫧 例句 : How do you account for the sudden drop in sales?
    • 🌈 同义 : explain, give an explanation for, justify
  • 💫 释义 : 占 ( 比例、数量 )
    • 🫧 例句 : Overseas sales account for over 60% of our total revenue.
    • 🌈 同义 : constitute, make up, represent, comprise
  • 💫 释义 : 说明 ( 去向 ), 对…负责
    • 🫧 例句 : All the missing files have now been accounted for.
    • 🌈 同义 : explain the whereabouts of, give a record of
  • 💫 释义 : ( 在战斗、事故中 ) 摧毁, 杀死
    • 🫧 例句 : The fighter pilot accounted for three enemy aircraft.
    • 🌈 同义 : | shoot down, eliminate, take out

on account of

  • 💫 释义 : 由于, 因为
    • 🫧 例句 : The match was postponed on account of the bad weather.
    • 🌈 同义 : because of, due to, owing to

take into account

  • 💫 释义 : 考虑; 顾及
    • 🫧 例句 : The judge took the defendant’s age into account when passing sentence.
    • 🌈 同义 : consider, take into consideration, bear in mind

Part 28

by all accounts

  • 💫 释义 : 根据各方面的说法; 大家都说
    • 🫧 例句 : By all accounts, he is an excellent and dedicated teacher.
    • 🌈 同义 : from what everyone says, reportedly, by all reports

call sb to account

  • 💫 释义 : 要求某人作出解释; 追究某人的责任
    • 🫧 例句 : The public is demanding that the government be called to account for its handling of the crisis.
    • 🌈 同义 : | hold someone accountable, demand an explanation from someone

settle accounts with sb

  • 💫 释义 : 与某人结清账目;
    ( 引申义 ) 与某人算账/报仇
    • 🫧 例句 : He returned to the city to settle accounts with his former partner.
    • 🌈 同义 : | get even with, get back at, square accounts with

a blow-by-blow account

  • 💫 释义 : ( 对事件 ) 极为详尽的叙述
    • 🫧 例句 : She gave us a blow-by-blow account of everything that happened at the meeting.
    • 🌈 同义 : a detailed description, a minute-by-minute report

go by

  • 💫 释义 : ( 时间 ) 流逝, 过去
    • 🫧 例句 : As time goes by, memories of the event begin to fade.
    • 🌈 同义 : ( of time ) to pass or elapse
  • 💫 释义 : 遵循, 依据
    • 🫧 例句 : When making important decisions, it’s best to go by the official guidelines.
    • 🌈 同义 : to be guided by, to follow the principles of
  • 💫 释义 : 经过, 路过
    • 🫧 例句 : I saw a delivery truck go by my office window just now.
    • 🌈 同义 : to pass ( a place )
  • 💫 释义 : 以…名字为人所知
    • 🫧 例句 : His passport says “Robert”, but he goes by “Bobby”.
    • 🌈 同义 : to be known by a particular name

approach… in… ways

  • 💫 释义 : 以…方式对待/处理…
    • 🫧 例句 : Different cultures approach the concept of time in fundamentally different ways.
    • 🌈 同义 : tackle… using… methods, handle… through… approaches, address… from… perspectives

committed to

  • 💫 释义 : 致力于; 投身于
    • 🫧 例句 : The company is committed to providing the highest quality of service.
    • 🌈 同义 : dedicated to, devoted to, pledged to

dedicated to

  • 💫 释义 : 专注于; 献身于
    • 🫧 例句 : She is dedicated to helping underprivileged children in her community.
    • 🌈 同义 : devoted to, committed to, focused on

devoted to

  • 💫 释义 : 奉献给; 忠于
    • 🫧 例句 : He has a section in his library devoted to historical biographies.
    • 🌈 同义 : dedicated to, reserved for, committed to

get up to speed

  • 💫 释义 : 使熟悉情况; 跟上进度
    • 🫧 例句 : The new manager spent her first week getting up to speed with the company’s projects.
    • 🌈 同义 : to reach a required standard of efficiency or knowledge, to become current or informed

Part 29

bring sb/sth up to speed

  • 💫 释义 : 使某人/某事物熟悉情况; 使跟上进度
    • 🫧 例句 : Can you bring me up to speed on what was decided at the meeting?
    • 🌈 同义 : to provide someone with the latest information, to make something modern or efficient

pearls of wisdom

  • 💫 释义 : 智慧的结晶; 精辟的见解 ( 常带幽默或讽刺 )
    • 🫧 例句 : We gathered around the old sailor, eager to hear his pearls of wisdom about life at sea.
    • 🌈 同义 : wise and insightful remarks, words of wisdom ( often used humorously )

out there

  • 💫 释义 : 存在的; 可获得的; 在外部世界的
    • 🫧 例句 : There are many great opportunities out there if you know where to look.
    • 🌈 同义 : in existence, available, in the wider world
  • 💫 释义 : 另类的; 非传统的
    • 🫧 例句 : His latest artistic creation is really out there and hard for most people to understand.
    • 🌈 同义 : unconventional, avant-garde, eccentric

prevent the need for

  • 💫 释义 : 避免对…的需求; 防患于未然
    • 🫧 例句 : Regular maintenance can prevent the need for costly repairs in the future.
    • 🌈 同义 : avoid the necessity of, make something unnecessary, obviate the requirement for

It is A more than B

  • 💫 释义 : 与其说是B, 不如说是A
    • 🫧 例句 : His success was a result of luck more than careful planning.
    • 🌈 同义 : rather A than B, not so much B as A

It is less B, more A

  • 💫 释义 : 与其说是B, 不如说是A ( 另一种强调说法 )
    • 🫧 例句 : The room’s style was less formal, more cozy and inviting.
    • 🌈 同义 : better described as A rather than B

not so much B as A

  • 💫 释义 : 与其说是B, 不如说是A
    • 🫧 例句 : He is not so much a genius as a very hard worker.
    • 🌈 同义 : it would be more accurate to say A than B

stick to

  • 💫 释义 : 坚持; 遵守
    • 🫧 例句 : If you stick to your study plan, you will see good results.
    • 🌈 同义 : adhere to, persist with, keep to, abide by

stick with

  • 💫 释义 : 继续坚持; 不放弃
    • 🫧 例句 : Learning a language is hard, but stick with it and you’ll get there.
    • 🌈 同义 : continue with, persevere with, not abandon

day in and day out

  • 💫 释义 : 日复一日; 一天又一天 ( 常指单调重复 )
    • 🫧 例句 : She practiced the piano day in and day out to prepare for the competition.
    • 🌈 同义 : every day without exception, continuously and often monotonously

Part 30

day after day

  • 💫 释义 : 一天又一天 ( 强调重复和时间的流逝 )
    • 🫧 例句 : Day after day, the scientists conducted the same experiment, hoping for a breakthrough.
    • 🌈 同义 : for many days in succession, repeatedly over time

all year round

  • 💫 释义 : 一年到头; 全年
    • 🫧 例句 : The swimming pool is open all year round.
    • 🌈 同义 : throughout the entire year, in every season

work on

  • 💫 释义 : 致力于; 努力做 ( 改进、完成某事 )
    • 🫧 例句 : She is working on a new novel these days.
    • 🌈 同义 : be engaged in, devote effort to, apply oneself to
  • 💫 释义 : 努力说服或影响
    • 🫧 例句 : I’ll work on my father to let us use the car tonight.
    • 🌈 同义 : try to persuade or influence
  • 💫 释义 : 对…产生作用/影响
    • 🫧 例句 : This medicine works on the central nervous system.
    • 🌈 同义 : have an effect on, act upon

one day at a time

  • 💫 释义 : 一次一天; 循序渐进
    • 🫧 例句 : Dealing with a big project can be overwhelming, so just take it one day at a time.
    • 🌈 同义 : dealing with each day’s problems as they come, without worrying about the future

step by step

  • 💫 释义 : 一步一步地; 逐步地
    • 🫧 例句 : Let’s approach this complex problem step by step.
    • 🌈 同义 : gradually and methodically, proceeding one stage at a time

little by little

  • 💫 释义 : 一点一点地; 逐渐地
    • 🫧 例句 : Little by little, her English improved through daily practice.
    • 🌈 同义 : gradually, by small degrees, bit by bit

avoid doing sth

  • 💫 释义 : 避免做某事
    • 🫧 例句 : You should avoid making the same mistake again.
    • 🌈 同义 : refrain from doing, keep from doing

avoid the issue/topic

  • 💫 释义 : 回避问题/话题
    • 🫧 例句 : He always avoids the issue when I ask about his plans.
    • 🌈 同义 : evade, sidestep, skirt around

avoid like the plague

  • 💫 释义 : 像避瘟疫一样避开; 极力避免
    • 🫧 例句 : After the scandal, everyone avoided him like the plague.
    • 🌈 同义 : stay completely away from, shun

take action

  • 💫 释义 : 采取行动
    • 🫧 例句 : We must take action before it’s too late.
    • 🌈 同义 : act, do something
